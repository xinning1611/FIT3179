Foreign Arrivals in Malaysia

Analysis of Trend From 2020 to 2024

World Distribution of Foreign Arrivals to Malaysia

Gender Breakdown of Top 10 Arrival Countries

Most foreign visitors come from Asian countries, except for Australia. Singapore has the highest total number of visitors to Malaysia at 34,496,707, contributing a significant portion of the total foreign arrivals to Malaysia.

The gender distribution among visitors from the top 10 countries that have highest visitor counts to Malaysia is relatively balanced. However, there are notable disparities for India and Bangladesh, where the number of male visitors significantly exceeds the number of female visitors.

You may hover over to the bars to view arrival counts for male and female for different countries.

Popular State for Foreign Arrivals in Malaysia

There is a total of 70,755,895 foreign arrivals from 2020 to 2024. The largest portion of foreign visitors visited Johor, with a total of 39,921,317 visitors. Other states such as Selangor, Sarawak, and Kedah also show high number of foreign visitors.

Pahang and Terengganu have the lowest share of foreign arrivals, with only 18,038 and 263 respectively, therefore they are hardly visible in the chart.

Select a state on the legend to view the state sector.

State Popularity Rankings Over Years (2020 to 2024)

Johor and Selangor consistently ranked as the top two states for foreign arrivals throughout the years, reflecting their status as a top destination for foreign arrivals. Although there was a drop in number in 2021 for Johor, but it still remain a high foreign visitors number throughout the years.

There were fluctuates in most rankings in 2020, which is impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic as many states show a decline in their rankings. There is not much changes between 2022 and 2024 where most rankings are remained.

Select a state from the legend to view its ranking, or you may use the slider to view different rankings across the years.

Monthly Foreign Arrivals by State

Most states have a peak in foreign arrivals in January, possibly driven by holidays, festivals, and favorable weather conditions. In contrast, July has the lowest visitors count which potentially due to weather patterns and lack of global holidays from other countries. Visitor numbers rise again toward the end of the year which is likely due to year-end holidays.

Exceptions such as Negeri Sembilan, Pahang, and Terengganu, follow a different pattern, with peaks in March and May.
